Interactive demo of salt-and-pepper and Gaussian noise, with median and Gaussian blur filters to remove them

  • Salt-and-pepper noise randomly flips scattered pixels to pure black or white — often from sensor errors or transmission glitches.
  • Gaussian noise adds small random variation to every pixel, drawn from a bell curve — typical of sensor/electronic noise.
  • Median filtering (replace each pixel with the median of its neighborhood) removes salt-and-pepper noise well since outliers get discarded rather than averaged in.
  • Gaussian blur smooths Gaussian noise well, but it averages salt-and-pepper outliers into neighbors instead of removing them — try both filters on both noise types.
